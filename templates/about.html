The majority of these genes are assessed for variants within all coding exons (+/- 20bp in the nearby flanking regions). Exons 12-15 of PMS2 and homopolymer regions outside of the coding regions cannot be reliably assessed with standard target enrichment protocols. For the CDK4, MITF, POLD1 and POLE genes, the elevated risk of cancer is associated with distinct functional genomic regions. The complete coding sequences of these genes are not reported, but instead only the following regions: CDK4 - chr12:g.58145429-58145431 (codon 24), MITF - chr3:g.70014091 (including c.952G>A), POLD1 - chr19:g.50909713 (including c.1433G>A) and POLE - chr12:g.133250250 (including c.1270C>G). In EPCAM, only large deletions and duplications including the 3â€™ end of the gene are reported. These are the only variants known to silence the MSH2 gene and therefore increase risk of associated cancer. GREM1 is only analyzed for duplications in the upstream regulatory region.

The 10,000 participants were split into two halves of 5,000 participants each. The two halves were sequenced in different labs, and covered slightly different regions of some genes with slightly different coverage statistics.

Coverage and read depth

  • Full sequencing and large rearrangements of all genes analyzed
  • Minimum read depth: 20X (>99% at >50X)
  • Median read depth: 250X (up to >1000X)
  • Intronic coverage: +/- 20bp, as well as intronic tiling

Reference genome

After alignment to reference genome GRCh37.p12 (hg19), low quality and duplicate reads are removed, remaining reads are aligned with BWA, and variants are detected with GATK Haplotypecaller. Structural variants (CNVs) are called based on a read depth analysis over the whole reported panel, along with any split reads detected.

What is detected?

Single nucleotide substitutions, small deletions and insertions, copy number variations and inversions located in the DNA coding sequences, nearby flanking regions (+/- 20bp) and known splice regions in the genes targeted by the Color panel.

What isn't detected?

Chromosomal aneuploidy or complex gene rearrangements such as translocations and large insertions. Mosaicism not reliably detected. The sensitivity to detect deletions, insertions, and inversions between approximately 40-250bp might be reduced.
